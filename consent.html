Concerned about consent?

Worried about an incident which you think may have been non-consensual?

Whether it's about you or someone you know, there's a variety of people you can talk to.

PEERS IN COLLEGE

  • FRIEND

    Of course, whether you feel comfortable raising an incident with a friend is up to you. We just wanted to make it clear that a friend can speak to any of the other people listed on this poster on your behalf.

  • FIRST RESPONDERS

    these are members of the JCR trained to receive disclosures of sexual violence. They can signpost you to different services and support you in contacting them. Entz reps are all trained first responders, so should an incident arise at a bop you can talk to one of them.

  • PEER SUPPORTERS

    whilst not all peer supporters are first responder trained, they all have active listening training so are a good port of call for any support you may need.

  • JCR WOMEN*'S REP

    first responder trained and with a good knowledge of issues around consent as they are responsible for running consent workshops in college.

  • WELFARE REPS

    the JCR welfare reps are both peer support and first responder trained, but as a bonus they have a deeper knowledge and understanding of college welfare structures.

STAFF IN COLLEGE

  • COLLEGE NURSE

    the nurse can of course help with any physical medical concerns, but is also trained to support anyone who needs to make a disclosure and can signpost you to support. She can also talk to other staff in college such as tutors on your behalf.

  • JUNIOR DEAN

    junior deans are graduate students with welfare training who are on call for emergencies. They are an important part of the college welfare team and are situated in different parts of Oxford, so there will be one near you if you are in college accommodation.

  • PORTERS

    porters are first aid trained and on call in the lodge at all times, so in an emergency situation you can contact them. They are also able to remove people from college property if you need that to happen.

  • WOMEN'S TUTOR

    the women's tutor at Hertford is the lead fellow for sexual harassment (regardless of your gender). She is very friendly and very supportive, and can speak to other tutors for you if you need.

  • DEAN

    the dean is the person responsible overall for welfare in college. They are able to refer you to sources of help, throughout the university and in college.

UNIVERSITY SERVICES

  • UNIVERSITY ADVICE SERVICE

    this service is just a great resource for literally anything, but the university recommends them if you are concerned about an incident of sexual assault or sexual violence. They are contactable on advice@ousu.ox.ac.uk and 01865 288466

  • HARASSMENT ADVISERS

    the university also has highly trained harassment advisers. You can contact them on harassment.line@admin.ox.ac.uk or 01865 270760

  • DIRECTOR OF STUDENT WELFARE AND SUPPORT SERVICES OFFICER

    the highest up person you can take something to inside the university. Contactable on 01865 280444 or director.swss@admin.ox.ac.uk

  • COUNSELLING

    for more long term support, including if you are supporting someone else after an incident, you can make appointments with the counselling service on counselling@admin.ox.ac.uk or 01865 270300

EXTERNAL SERVICES

  • OSARCC

    Oxfordshire Sexual Abuse & Rape Crisis Centre. A support centre in Oxford for women and girls. You can contact them on support@osarc.org.uk, 0800 783 6294 and 01865 726295.

  • POLICE

    of course if you wish to you can take any incident to the police. This can be a difficult process to go through, so do consider getting support from one of the other contact points on this poster so that you don't have to face it alone.
